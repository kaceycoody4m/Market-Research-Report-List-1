<p><p>The passenger car interior leather market consists of two main types: genuine leather and synthetic leather. Genuine leather is derived from animal hides, prized for its durability, luxurious feel, and breathability, making it a preferred choice for high-end vehicles. In contrast, synthetic leather, often made from polyurethane or polyvinyl chloride, offers a more affordable, environmentally friendly alternative. It mimics the appearance of genuine leather while being easier to clean and maintain, catering to a broader range of consumer preferences and budgets.</p></p>
